This site exists to help community college students plan for a tricky
moment: AI is already changing which entry-level jobs are easy to get. We
built it to lay out the real evidence — not just the scary headlines —
and to give you a clear next step, whether that's a backup plan, a skill
to learn, or a bill to watch.

## Our mission

We have one goal: help you make an informed decision, not a scared one.
That means:

- Showing you the real data on how AI is affecting entry-level jobs right
  now, not just predictions.
- Being upfront when researchers disagree, or when the picture isn't
  clear yet.
- Giving you practical, low-cost next steps — a backup plan, ways to
  build AI skills, and how to stay informed on policy — that work for a
  busy student, not just someone with a lot of free time and money.

We're not trying to talk you out of your major or your interests. We're
trying to help you go in with your eyes open.

## Who made this

This is an independent, non-commercial educational project — not
affiliated with any college, employer, or AI company, and not sponsored
by anyone with a financial stake in this topic.

One example on this site uses real program names from Stark State
College to show what "AI exposure" looks like in a concrete way. That
doesn't mean this site is affiliated with Stark State — it's a real,
public list of associate-degree and certificate programs used to make an
abstract statistic feel real. The same kind of exposure applies broadly
to similar programs at other community colleges.

## How we handle sources and bias

Every statistic or claim on this site is tied to a specific, named
source — you can check any of them on the [References](/references/)
page. When a source has a financial or institutional stake in the answer
(for example, research an AI company published about its own product),
we say so right next to that source, not just in a footnote.

We also try to include reasonable disagreement, not just the most
alarming numbers. See [The Evidence](/the-evidence/) for both the data on
displacement and the strongest counterarguments to it.
