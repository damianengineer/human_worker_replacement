Where this story is

One way to hold this whole site in your head: four moments on one timeline, from settled history to open speculation. The further right you look, the less anyone actually knows — and that's exactly why the two kinds of action below run on two different clocks.

  1. The Leader of the Luddites (1812) — Walker & Knight (publisher; artist unknown), 1812

    The past

    Documented

    In 1813, seventeen men were hanged for breaking the machines that took their jobs. By 2025, Amazon ran roughly a million warehouse robots. Technology reshaping who gets hired isn't new — we have four earlier cases to learn from.

    Read the history

  2. An Amazon Robotics drive-unit robot — Auledas, 2025

    Right now

    Measured

    Since ChatGPT launched, employment for young workers in AI-exposed jobs has fallen about 13–16% — concentrated in entry-level hiring. Not mass layoffs: a door quietly closing.

    See the evidence

  3. Near future

    Forecast — contested

    AGI — AI that matches capable humans at most mental work — doesn't exist, and experts disagree on when or whether it will. But it's what the major labs say they're building, so it belongs in your planning as a possibility, not a promise.

    What AGI actually means

  4. Open future

    Speculative

    If machines ever do most of the work, who gets paid? That question — not science fiction — is what's behind the UBI and sovereign wealth fund proposals in Congress right now.
