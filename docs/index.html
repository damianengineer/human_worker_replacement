AI & Your First Job

Getting a first job after graduation is getting harder in some fields — and the data says AI is part of why. This site pulls together peer-reviewed research, data from the two biggest AI labs, and one independent, skeptical economist's review, so you can make an informed plan instead of guessing.

The short version

Employment for young workers (22–25) in AI-exposed jobs has fallen about 13–16% since ChatGPT launched — concentrated in entry-level hiring, not senior layoffs. That's the clearest single finding in the data, but it's far from the whole picture.

Source: Brynjolfsson, E., Chandar, B., & Chen, R., 2025; Massenkoff, M., & McCrory, P., 2026

Background before you dive in

Two pages will help as you read the rest of the site. History looks at four earlier moments — the Luddites, telephone operators, office computerization, Amazon's warehouses — where new technology reshaped who got hired. They're analogies, not proof of what AI will do. And since this topic comes with real jargon, the Glossary defines every term in plain language and traces each one back to where it actually came from — not just its most recent headline.
